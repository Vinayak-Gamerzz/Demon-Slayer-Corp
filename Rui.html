About

Rui is the Lower Rank Five of the Twelve Kizuki and one of the first powerful demons Tanjiro faces. Although he appears calm and childlike, Rui is incredibly cruel and believes that fear and obedience are the foundation of a true family

Before becoming a demon, Rui was a sickly child who longed for a normal life. After Muzan turned him into a demon, he lost the people who truly loved him. Unable to understand genuine family bonds, he created a fake family of demons and forced them to obey him. His tragic past makes him one of the most memorable early villains in the series

    • Appearance

    Rui has pale skin, short black hair, and red spider web markings across his face and body. His white kimono and emotionless expression give him a quiet appearance that contrasts sharply with his terrifying powers

    Personality

    Rui longs for the warmth of a real family but has forgotten what love truly means. He believes loyalty can be forced through fear and punishment, treating the demons around him as replaceable members of his perfect family

    Despite his cruelty, Rui's past reveals a lonely child who simply wanted to be loved. His story adds an emotional layer to his character and makes his defeat one of the most heartbreaking moments in the series

    Fighting Style

    Rui controls razor sharp threads that are almost impossible to see. He uses them to attack, defend, and trap his enemies from a distance. His strongest threads are powerful enough to slice through stone and even break swords

    • Legacy

    Rui is remembered as one of the most emotional villains in Demon Slayer. His tragic childhood and desperate search for a family make him far more than just another enemy. His story teaches that real family is built through love, sacrifice, and trust rather than control and fear
